External Review & Filing a Complaint in {{ state.name }}

If your health insurer upholds a denial after your internal appeal, you can usually ask for an independent external review and file a complaint with your state regulator. Here is the general path in {{ state.name }}.

1Finish your internal appeal first

External review normally happens after you complete your health plan's internal appeal (sometimes called a grievance). Keep copies of every denial letter and decision — you will typically need the plan's final adverse determination before an external review can begin.

Start your appeal with our free tool, review the appeal steps and resources, or chat with our AI assistant for help preparing your internal appeal.

2Request an independent external review

Who runs the review: {% if state.external_review %}{{ state.external_review.review_type_label }}{% else %}Varies — verify with your state Department of Insurance{% endif %}.

For most state-regulated (fully insured) plans, {{ state.name }} offers its own external review, where an independent organization — not your insurer — re-examines the denial. Some plans instead use the federal external review process administered by the U.S. Department of Health & Human Services (HHS). This commonly applies to self-funded employer ("self-insured") plans and certain other coverage. Which process covers you depends on your plan type, so confirm the right path before you file.

{% if state.external_review.info_url %} {{ state.name }} external review details {% endif %}
3File a complaint with the {{ state.insurance_department.name }}

You can also file a complaint with the {{ state.insurance_department.name }}, the agency that regulates insurers in {{ state.name }}. A complaint can prompt the regulator to review how your insurer handled the denial, and staff can often explain your external review rights.

Deadlines vary. Time limits to request external review or file a complaint differ by state, plan type, and whether your situation is urgent — and they can be short. Do not wait: verify the exact deadline that applies to you with the {{ state.insurance_department.name }} using the contact details above before you file.
