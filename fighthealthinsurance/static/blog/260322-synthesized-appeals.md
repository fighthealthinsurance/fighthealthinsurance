## What Are Synthesized Appeals?

Our system has always used multiple AI models working together — think of them as a team of specialists, each bringing a different perspective to your appeal. One model might zero in on the [medical necessity](https://www.healthcare.gov/glossary/medically-necessary/) argument. Another might dig up the perfect policy citation. A third might nail the tone and structure that gets a reviewer's attention.

What's new is the **synthesis layer**. After all models contribute their best work, an additional step reviews every draft, pulls out the strongest arguments, the most relevant citations, and the most persuasive language — and combines it all into one appeal letter.

Think of it like a greatest hits album, but for your insurance appeal.

## Why Multiple Perspectives Matter

Insurance denials are complicated. They involve medical terminology, plan-specific policy language, regulatory requirements, and sometimes just plain bureaucratic confusion. No single approach catches everything.

By generating multiple drafts and then synthesizing them, we increase the chances that your appeal:

- Includes the most relevant medical evidence
- Cites the right plan provisions and regulations
- Makes a clear, persuasive case for why the denial should be overturned
- Doesn't leave strong arguments on the table

It's the difference between asking one person for advice and getting input from an entire team — then having someone put together the best of what everyone said.

## Greener Than You Think

You might wonder about the energy cost of running multiple AI models. Fair question. The entire process — generating multiple drafts and synthesizing them — uses less power than playing a video game on a modern computer for a day. We're not boiling the ocean here.

## Important: Always Check Your Work

Here's the part where we get serious for a second. This feature is **experimental**. AI can and does make mistakes — sometimes subtle ones. That's true for synthesized appeals, and it's true for everything else our system generates.

**Always review your appeal before sending it.** Make sure the facts are right, the citations are accurate, and the letter reflects your actual situation. Your appeal is yours — we're here to give you a strong starting draft, not the final word.
